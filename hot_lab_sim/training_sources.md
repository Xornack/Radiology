### Key facts from NMSS-ISG-03 (April 2025, ML25051A034 — the PDF in this repo)

- **Online training explicitly counts** toward classroom/laboratory hours: NRC "will broadly interpret 'classroom and laboratory training' to include various types of instruction, including online training," when clock-hours are met and subject matter relates to radiation safety / safe handling (ISG p. 17).
- **NRC may audit the training entity**: for online programs, NRC "may contact the training entity to discuss the training program components (course outline, test, mechanism to ensure that the proposed individual completed the training and test)." → The platform must be able to export a syllabus/course outline, per-module learning objectives, exam content, and completion-verification evidence on demand.
- **Hours = time engaged in the learning activity** (supports watched-seconds-based hour crediting). An hour may be counted in only one category (classroom/lab vs work experience).
- Clinical activities (reading scans, case review) may count toward the 700 total as supervised work experience but **never** toward classroom/laboratory hours.
- Preceptor attestation (Form 313A Part II) is required for alternate-pathway AUs; **not** required for board-certification-pathway AUs (post-2019 rule). The residency program director may serve as preceptor.
- Recentness: T&E must be within 7 years of application (10 CFR 35.59) — fine for residents.
- Current documentation reference: **NUREG-1556 Vol. 9 Revision 3 (Sept 2019), Appendix D** (plan.md previously cited Rev 2).
- Authorization flows through the **licensee** (license amendment via Form 313A); the NRC issues nothing to the individual, and individuals should not send T&E documentation to the NRC.
